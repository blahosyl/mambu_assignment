Overview

A Credit Arrangement is the maximum amount that a client (an individual, a group or a company) can take out in loans and overdrafts. It is similar in function to Maximum Exposure defined in “General Settings” → “Internal Controls”. The main difference between a Credit Arrangement and Maximum Exposure is that a Credit Arrangement can be extended separately and vary from client to client, whereas Maximum Exposure is a validation that is pre-set at the system level and it is the same for every client. 

A client or a group may have multiple Credit Arrangements with specific loan and overdraft accounts linked to them. Each Credit Arrangement can have its own constraints, including a Maximum Credit Exposure amount and validity dates. The parameters of all loan and overdraft accounts linked to a Credit Arrangement have to be within the constraints defined for that specific Credit Agreement.

This functionality has been added to Mambu to give you more flexibility in creating Credit Arrangements by combining multiple accounts, including loan and overdraft accounts, into one agreement.

Possible Statuses of Credit Arrangements

In Mambu, Credit Arrangements are assigned transition statuses from “Pending Approval” to “Approved”. 

To assign a default transition status to all new Credit Agreements, go to “Administration” → “Internal Controls” →   “New Credit Arrangement Initial Status”. The default transition status of a new Credit Agreement can either be set to “Approved” or “Pending Approval”.  

Insert relevant screenshot here

If the configuration above is set to “Pending Approval”, then all new Credit Arrangements will be created with this status and require user approval. In order to approve a new Credit Arrangement, a user must have the permission “Approve Credit Arrangements” enabled.

The table below lists additional actions that can be performed on Credit Arrangements that have the status “Pending Approval”, along with the permissions necessary to carry them out.


Action Permission required
Undo Approval Undo Approval Credit Arrangement
Close – Withdraw Withdraw Credit Arrangements
Undo Withdraw Undo Withdraw Credit Arrangements
Close – Reject Reject Credit Arrangements
Undo – Reject Undo Reject Credit Arrangements


If a Credit Arrangement has the status “Pending Approval”, it is not possible to add accounts to it: the Credit Agreement has to be approved first.


Every time a Credit Arrangement's transitions status is changed, the change is logged.


Rejecting or withdrawing a Credit Arrangement is only possible if it has the status “Pending Approval”. Credit Arrangements that have the status “Active” cannot be withdrawn or rejected. Furthermore, it is not possible to edit the terms of Credit Arrangements with the status “Closed-Withdrawn” or “Closed-Rejected”.

Credit Arrangements with the status “Pending Approval” can be deleted, since cannot have any accounts connected to them. This is also true for Credit Arrangements with the status “Approved” that do not have any accounts linked to them. Once accounts are added to a Credit Arrangement, it can no longer be deleted.

Creating a New Credit Arrangement

Credit Arrangements can be defined for each individual client, therefore, they can be created directly in the client’s profile. The option to create a new Credit Arrangement is available under “New Account” → “Credit Arrangement”. 



When creating a new Credit Arrangement, the following parameters have to be specified: 
  • Amount – the Maximum Credit Exposure of all loan and overdraft accounts balances linked to the Credit Arrangement. This means that the sum of original loan amounts and total overdraft balances may not exceed the Credit Arrangement Amount. 

 Please note that only outstanding funds of accounts that are linked to the Credit Agreement in questions are considered in the validations of the Credit Arrangement's exposure limit.
 
  • ID – each Credit Arrangement is given a unique, automatically generated identifier. The identifier template is predefined at the database level and can only be changed by the Mambu technical team (by contacting the Mambu support team). 
 Please note that it is possible to edit and change the ID manually when creating or editing a Credit Arrangement. 

The two statements above seem contradictory

  • Start Date – each Credit Arrangement has a start date (“Starts From”). Any accounts connected to the Credit Agreement can only be disbursed (in the case of loan accounts) or activated (in the case of overdraft accounts) after this date. 
  • End Date – each Credit Arrangement has an end date (“Valid Until”). Any loan accounts connected to the Credit Agreement can only be disbursed if the expected maturity as per the schedule is before the end date. Overdraft accounts can only be approved or added if the expiry date is before this end date.
  • Notes – this field allows you to include any additional information about the Credit Arrangement.

Can the disbursement date of an account be the same as the start date of the Credit Agreement? The formulation above implies that it cannot, whereas later in the article (Editing a Credit Agreement), the formulation allows for this configuration. The same goes for maturity dates of accounts and the end date of the Credit Agreement.


  Additional notes: 
  • loans with multiple tranches will only have the first tranche dates validated against the Credit Arrangement constraints, since the other tranches are not firmly confirmed when the account is created
  • clients can have more than one Credit Arrangement
  • clients may have some accounts linked to a Credit Arrangement, while having accounts outside of the Credit Arrangement at the same time.

Viewing a Credit Arrangement 

Once a Credit Arrangement is created, it is displayed under a separate tab on the client overview page (just like loan accounts). Click on the tab to see the Credit Arrangement details, the list of all the accounts managed under the Credit Arrangement, to add notes or change the Credit Arrangement parameters. A full audit trail of all actions performed is logged and visible under “Activities”. 

The information available in the Overview section of the Credit Arrangement tab include: 
  • Available Credit Amount – the Amount defined in the Credit Arrangement (Maximum Credit Exposure) less the sum of all loan and overdraft amounts of accounts linked to the Credit Arrangement. The Available Credit amount always shows the unused part of the Credit Arrangement limit. 
  • Approved Credit Amount – the sum of all loan and overdraft amounts of approved and active accounts linked to the Credit Arrangement.
  • Accounts Summary – information about the accounts linked to the Credit Arrangement.
  • General information and Details – detailed information about the Credit Arrangement parameters. 
  • Status – the current status of the Credit Arrangement: “Pending Approval”, “Approved”, “Active” or “Closed”. A Credit Arrangement’s status is changed to “Active” when accounts are added to it.
  • Approval Date – the date when the Credit Arrangement was approved.

Adding Accounts to a Credit Arrangement 

The possibility to add loan or overdraft accounts to a Credit Arrangement is defined in the settings of the respective product, with 3 available alternatives:
  • Optional –​ accounts in this product group can be managed as part of a Credit Arrangement, but it is not compulsory to have a Credit Arrangement in order to open an account within this product group
  • Required – ​accounts in this product group can’t be approved if they’re not associated with a Credit Arrangement
  • Restricted – ​accounts in this product group cannot be managed under Credit Arrangements

These settings are available under “Loan Products” → “Loan Amount” and under “Deposit Products” → “Overdraft Conditions”. 

Insert relevant screenshot here

After a Credit Arrangement is created, no account is associated with it by default. Only already existing loan and overdraft accounts can be added to a Credit Arrangement. In other words, the loan or overdraft account has to be created first, and then it can be linked to a Credit Arrangement. 

Users can add active accounts to a Credit Arrangement under the following conditions:
  • Loan Accounts ­can be added if the disbursement date is after the Credit Arrangement start date and the maturity date is before the Credit Arrangement expiry date. In addition, the sum of loan amounts (original funds) cannot exceed the Credit Arrangement's Maximum Exposure Amount. 
  • Overdraft Accounts ­can be added if the overdraft expiry date is before the Credit Arrangement expiry date and if the overdraft balance doesn’t exceed the Credit Arrangement's Maximum Exposure Amount. Overdrafts that don’t have an expiry date or a Maximum Overdraft Amount cannot be added to Credit Arrangements.

To add an account to a Credit Arrangement, go to “More” → “Add Account”. You can only choose from the list of accounts that satisfy the all of the following conditions: 
  • they are loan or deposit accounts allowing overdrafts 
  • their product setup permits adding them to Credit Arrangements (“Optional” or “Required”)
  • they already existing in the client's portfolio 
  • they have one of the following statuses: “Partial Application”, “Pending Approval” “Approved” or “Active”
  • they are not yet assigned to any Credit Arrangements
  • their terms fall within the constraints of the Credit Arrangement

Once an account is linked to a Credit Arrangement, the link will be visible in the account’s overview.

Removing Accounts from a Credit Arrangement 

Accounts that are not yet closed can be removed (unlinked) from a Credit Arrangement as long as the product setup does not require the account to be linked to a Credit Arrangement.

The option “Remove Account from a Credit Arrangement” is available under the “More” button. This displays all loan and overdraft accounts that have previously been linked to the Credit Arrangement.

Removing an account from a Credit Arrangement will update the Available Credit Amount Balances and Approved Credit Amount.

Rescheduling/Refinancing Accounts under a Credit Arrangement

Accounts that are linked to a Credit Arrangement can only be rescheduled/refinanced into a different product if the target product allows for its accounts to be linked to a Credit Arrangement. The constraints on the balance, start and end dates also need to be met.

Editing a Credit Arrangement

Credit Arrangements can be adjusted any time, even after accounts have been linked to them. For example, the Credit Arrangement Amount can be increased and the “Valid Until” date extended.

The parameters of the Credit Agreement can be edited by clicking on the “Edit” button in the top right corner. Editing a Credit Arrangement allows you to change the following parameters:
  • Amount – can be higher, lower or equal to the sum of total loan & overdraft amounts of accounts linked to the Credit Arrangement. When the Credit Arrangement amount is below the Exposure Limit, the available credit balance becomes negative. All changes made to the Credit Arrangement limit are logged in Mambu.
 
  • ID

  • Start Date – cannot be changed to a date after the earliest disbursement / activation date of all loan and overdraft accounts linked to the Credit Arrangement
  • End Date – cannot be changed to a date earlier than the latest maturity / overdraft expiry date of all the loan and overdraft accounts linked to the Credit Arrangement

Closing a Credit Arrangement

Credit Arrangements that are past their validity date or are not needed anymore can be closed.
This option is only available for Credit Arrangements that do not have any open accounts linked to them.

When a Credit Arrangement is closed, it will not be possible to add accounts to it, remove accounts from it, to edit or delete the Credit Agreement.

Closed Credit Arrangements will be displayed under the “Closed Accounts” section of the client’s page.

Accounts linked to a closed Credit Arrangement cannot be reopened unless the Credit Arrangement itself is reopened first.

Deleting a Credit Arrangement

A Credit Arrangement can only be deleted from the system if no accounts are associated with it. Once the accounts linked to it have been activated, the Credit Arrangement cannot be deleted.

General comments

  • Visual marks on screenshots should also be unified: some use arrows, others use borders or highlights. The same goes for the annotation color: red and yellow are used in the same screenshot – one color should be used consistently.
